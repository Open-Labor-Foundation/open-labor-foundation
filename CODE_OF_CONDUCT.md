# Code of Conduct

## Who this is for

The Open Labor Foundation (OLF) exists to benefit workers. Everyone contributing to OLF repositories — through code, domain knowledge, governance input, or community participation — is expected to act in a way that serves that mission.

## Expected behavior

- Treat domain experts, engineers, and community members with respect
- Give honest, constructive feedback — on ideas, specs, and code
- Assume good faith; most people are here to build something useful
- Acknowledge when you are outside your area of knowledge
- Keep discussions focused on the work

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Undermining the collective model for private or extractive benefit
- Using the catalog, platform, or community resources to harm workers
- Coordinated efforts to capture the foundation for corporate or proprietary interests
- Intentional exclusion of domain experts who lack technical backgrounds

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all OLF project spaces — repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any community channels associated with the foundation.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by emailing **[conduct@openlabor.foundation](mailto:conduct@openlabor.foundation)** or by opening a private issue. Reports will be reviewed and acted on. Repeated or serious violations result in removal from the project.

## Foundation

This foundation exists because workers deserve infrastructure that belongs to them. Keep that in mind in every interaction here.
