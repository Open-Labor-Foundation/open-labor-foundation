# Contributing to the Open Labor Foundation

There are three ways to contribute: domain knowledge, ideas, and infrastructure. The first two require no technical background. The third is for engineers.

We are actively building a streamlined contribution interface using [commons-idea](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-idea), [commons-specs](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-specs), and [commons-artifacts](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-artifacts) — so that anyone can contribute without needing to navigate source code or understand the underlying tooling. That work is in progress. The paths below describe how the process is intended to work once it is complete, and how to contribute today if you are comfortable working directly with the repositories.

---

## Contribute your expertise

If you know a profession, a trade, or a specialized domain, you can contribute a specialist definition to [labor-commons](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/labor-commons) that makes your knowledge available to everyone on the platform.

You do not need to know how to code.

**How it works:**

Run [commons-idea](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-idea). Describe what you do — the problems you solve, the decisions you make, the knowledge you apply, what good work looks like in your field. commons-idea asks questions until it fully understands, shows you what it heard, and waits for your confirmation.

The output is a complete written record of your expertise. Submit it to [labor-commons](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/labor-commons) as a pull request.

Your contribution doesn't only serve people in your field. It serves every worker on the platform who ever needs that knowledge — regardless of their own profession or background.

---

## Contribute an idea

commons-idea isn't only for specialist knowledge. If you have any idea for a tool, a workflow, a process, a piece of software, or something you want to exist — commons-idea can help you build it.

**What comes out:**

- A **written record** of your idea — what it is, who it's for, what it does. Contribute it to [commons-specs](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-specs) so others can learn from and build on it.
- A **working version** that runs on your device — unzip it, click one file, it opens. Contribute it to [commons-artifacts](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-artifacts) so others can use what you made.

---

## Contribute to the software stack

For engineers contributing to the platform repositories:

- All software repositories are licensed under AGPL-3.0 — contributions must be made under the same license
- Open an issue before starting significant work
- PRs should be focused and include tests where applicable
- [commons-keeper](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-keeper) runs autonomously against labor-commons — human contributions take precedence and set the quality bar

---

## Contribute to foundation governance

The foundation's governance model — how collectives form, how decisions get made, how the maintenance model works — is actively being developed. If you want to participate in shaping it, open an issue to start the conversation.

---

## Code of conduct

This foundation exists to benefit workers. Contributions that serve extractive interests — corporate capture, proprietary forks that take without giving back, undermining the collective model — are outside the scope of this project.

See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) for the full expectations.
